Kitchen Sink Product Design

Problem Statement & User Group

The project began with the question: How might we keep shared kitchen sinks in dorms usable and cleanly to ease roommate tension? This was designed around undergraduate students—our identified user group—who often struggle with maintaining cleanliness in shared living spaces. The sink, in particular, was a major source of frustration and tension among roommates.

Understanding the Problem

We approached the issue by breaking down the components of the problem using a fill-in-the-blank method. Through this, we discovered that the primary point of contention came from the build-up of dirty dishes and an unclean sink, both of which negatively affected roommate dynamics. These findings led us to focus on solutions that would encourage better habits and reduce conflict.

Exploration & Design Direction

Initial ideations explored solutions inspired by hostile architecture—designs meant to discourage certain behaviors by making them uncomfortable. However, we realized that making the sink unusable would only create more issues. Other ideas, like a faucet extender, already existed and didn’t solve all the key problems. Instead, we chose to design a product that could enhance the functionality of existing sinks without requiring a full redesign.

Key Pain Points of the Sink

We identified four core issues that students faced with shared sinks:

  • Shallow sink depth
  • Inaccessible drain when dishes piled up
  • Poor faucet positioning
  • Flat bottoms that trapped food and grime

These pain points were varied, and while individual solutions existed, we wanted to create one unified product that could address as many of these problems as possible without replacing the sink.

Final Design

Our final design is a drainable basket suspended by tension rods, keeping dirty dishes organized and the sink accessible. It features:

  • Drain holes to prevent water buildup
  • A sliding and folding mechanism for flexibility and storage
  • A counterweight strap system for added support

This system fits most standardized sinks, stores easily, and helps maintain a clean environment. One unique feature is the adjustable strap that allows the basket to fold into tiers—replacing our earlier mechanism that didn’t work well for students. Though it has some constraints (like limited support for large or heavy dishes), the design offers a simple, functional solution to a common dorm problem.
